About Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan, officially known as the Republic of Kazakhstan, is a transcontinental country predominantly located in Central Asia, with a small portion west of the Ural River in Europe. Covering a vast land area of 2,724,900 square kilometers (1,052,100 sq mi), it shares land borders with Russia to the north, China to the east, and Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan to the south. It also adjoins a significant part of the Caspian Sea in the southwest. Although Kazakhstan and Mongolia are only 37 kilometers apart, they do not share a border, separated by a small stretch of land between Russia and China.
Kazakhstan holds the title of the largest landlocked country in the world and is the ninth largest country overall. Despite its vast size, it has a population of just 18.8 million residents, resulting in one of the lowest population densities globally, with fewer than 6 people per square kilometer (15 people per sq mi). The capital city is Nur-Sultan, previously known as Astana, a change made in 1997 when the capital was moved from Almaty, the country’s largest city.
