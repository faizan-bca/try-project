About Georgia

Georgia, located at the eastern end of the Black Sea near the southern flanks of the Greater Caucasus Mountains, is bordered by Russia to the north, Azerbaijan to the east and southeast, Armenia and Turkey to the south, and the Black Sea to the west. It includes three ethnic regions: Abkhazia (Sokhumi), Ajaria (Batumi), and South Ossetia (Tskhinvali). The capital is Tbilisi. ... Georgia boasts a rich and ancient cultural heritage, with a remarkable medieval kingdom that peaked between the 10th and 13th centuries. After enduring Turkish and Persian rule, it was annexed by the Russian Empire in the 19th century. An independent Georgian state existed from 1918 to 1921 before becoming part of the Soviet Union. Georgia became a Soviet republic in 1936, experiencing modernization and diversification. It declared sovereignty on November 19, 1989, and independence on April 9, 1991, making it one of the most independence-minded republics of the former Soviet Union.
