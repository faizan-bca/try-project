About BELARUS

Belarus, formally called the Republic of Belarus, is a landlocked nation situated in Eastern Europe. Bordered by Russia to the east and northeast, Ukraine to the south, Poland to the west, and Lithuania and Latvia to the northwest, Belarus is often referred to as White Russia or White Ruthenia. The country covers 207,600 square kilometers (80,200 sq mi) and has a population of 9.4 million, making it the thirteenth-largest and twentieth-most populous country in Europe. Minsk, the capital and largest city, is at the heart of one of the world's most urbanized nations, with over 40% of its land area forested. Belarus declared independence on August 25, 1991, and in 2000, it signed a treaty with Russia to form the Union State, enhancing cooperation between the two nations. The borders of Belarus were largely established in 1939 after the Soviet invasion of Poland and finalized post-World War II. The country is divided into seven administrative regions and boasts a temperate-continental climate with hot summers (25-30°C) and long, cold winters (-5-0°C). ... The Belarusian national flag features a red-and-green design with a white-and-red ornament pattern, introduced in 2012. President Alexander Lukashenko explains that the red symbolizes freedom and the sacrifices made by the nation's forefathers, while the green signifies life. Belarus is renowned for its natural beauty, greenery, and diverse flora and fauna. Additionally, the country is famous for its delectable sweets and desserts, contributing to its rich cultural heritage.

Admission Process For MBBS In Belarus 2024

The admission process for MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) in Belarus may vary slightly among different universities, but generally, the following steps are involved. Keep in mind that it's crucial to check the specific requirements of the university you are interested in, as they may have unique criteria.

    Research and Choose a University:
  1. Look for universities in Belarus that offer MBBS programs. Some popular choices include Moscow State Medical University, Saint Petersburg State Medical University, and Kazan Federal University.
  2. Check the university's reputation, accreditation, and the medium of instruction (some programs are offered in English).
    3. Eligibility Criteria:
  3. Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria set by the chosen university. Typically, this includes a high school diploma or an equivalent qualification with a background in science (biology, chemistry, physics).
    4. Entrance Exams:
  4. Some universities may require you to take an entrance exam. The most common exam is the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) for Indian students. Other universities may have their own entrance exams.
    5. Application Process:
  5. Fill out the application form provided by the university. This can often be done online through the university's official website.
  6. repare the required documents, which may include academic transcripts, a copy of your passport, passport-sized photographs, a medical certificate, and proof of language proficiency (if the program is in English).
