About Tajikistan

Tajikistan, located in Central Asia, is bordered by China, Afghanistan, Uzbekistan, and Kyrgyzstan. Officially the Republic of Tajikistan, its capital is Dushanbe. Tajikistan became a separate country in 1991 after the Soviet Union's dissolution, with economic growth picking up post-1997. The government is a unitary dominant-party presidential constitutional republic, headed by a president and a prime minister. The people, known as Tajiks, speak Tajik, a Persian dialect, with Russian used for inter-ethnic communication. Islam is the predominant religion. Tajikistan is a member of international organizations like the UN, NATO, CIS, OSCE, OIC, ECO, SCO, and CSTO, actively participating in the Economic Cooperation Organization (ECO). The country is renowned for its mountains and lakes, with Gusthigiri, a form of wrestling, as the national sport. The currency is the Somoni. Tajikistan's climate is arid continental, featuring very cold winters and hot, sunny summers, with temperature and rainfall varying by altitude and slope exposure.

Admission Process For MBBS In Tajikistan 2024

The admission process for MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) in Tajikistan may vary slightly among different universities, but generally, the following steps are involved. Keep in mind that it's crucial to check the specific requirements of the university you are interested in, as they may have unique criteria.

    Research and Choose a University:
  1. Look for universities in Tajikistan that offer MBBS programs. Some popular choices include Moscow State Medical University, Saint Petersburg State Medical University, and Kazan Federal University.
  2. Check the university's reputation, accreditation, and the medium of instruction (some programs are offered in English).
    3. Eligibility Criteria:
  3. Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria set by the chosen university. Typically, this includes a high school diploma or an equivalent qualification with a background in science (biology, chemistry, physics).
    4. Entrance Exams:
  4. Some universities may require you to take an entrance exam. The most common exam is the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) for Indian students. Other universities may have their own entrance exams.
    5. Application Process:
  5. Fill out the application form provided by the university. This can often be done online through the university's official website.
  6. repare the required documents, which may include academic transcripts, a copy of your passport, passport-sized photographs, a medical certificate, and proof of language proficiency (if the program is in English).
